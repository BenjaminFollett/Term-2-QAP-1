Pallas cat bio


Habitat


The Pallas cat is a small little furball of a wild cat also known as Otocolobus manul. These cats typically like to live in a variety of areas such as montane shrublands and or grasslands, rocky outcrops or ravines in areas where theres snow. in a more specific sense they are found in Caucasus eastward to Central Asia and Mongolia and also parts of Dzungaria and the Tibetan Plateau.


Hunting and Dieting


Do not let these little cute fur-balls fool you they are highly speacialised predator when it comes to small mammals. The Pallas cats catches there preys by stalking or ambushing near exits of burrows. Its also sticks there paws in the prey burrows and pulls out rodents with its paws from the shallow burrows.

Here is a small list thats apart of the Palla cats diet.


  • Long-tailed ground squirrel
  • Flat-skulled shrew
  • Pallas's pika
  • Daurian pika
  • steppe pika
  • Daurian ground squirrel
  • Etc...

    • Characteristics


    These cats have light grey with pale yellowish or yellowish-reddish hues. some hair tips are white and some are blackish its fur is really dense some would call it puffy and its usually really gray. They also have some small black spots on there forhead and some zigzag lines on there cheeks. There tail is also really long and puffy with a black tip at the end of it.


    Threats


    Unfortunately just like every living thing on this planet these cats have theats to them. In China, Mongolia and Russia these cats were hunted for there pelt in large numbers. Herding dogs would also kill Palla cats in iran, Kazakhstan and Altai Republic. Till this day poaching Pallas cats for there pelt is still a ongoing problem.
